Who am I

Ever since I was a child, I had a natural curiosity for how things work. I would dismantle a toy that my father would buy me and challenge myself to assemble it again. By the age of 8, I learned about the router and the capabilities it brings with it. Growing up, I realized that I must explore deeper into the electronics and semi-conductors world.

Today, I am walking a path exploring what I was fantasizing about as a child, studying the fundamentals of electronics, digital logic, and circuits as a computer engineering student and the University of Guelph.

I participated in FRC 2017 and 2018 as part of the REBELS 2702 team. I participated in the Ontario Skills competition for robotics in 2018.

I worked as an IT analyst at Sanctuary Refugee Health Center. I was responsible for an improvement plan for the electronics equipment while saving 15% of the budget.

I spend some time with family and on personal projects to expand my knowledge in VHDL, digital logic, and CAD programs, like Solidworks.
